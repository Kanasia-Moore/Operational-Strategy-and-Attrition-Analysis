## Week 02 Reflection

This week shifted my perspective in a way I wasn’t expecting.

Going into this, I assumed that most of the issues related to attrition would come from the nature of the work itself — the physical strain, repetition, and workload. While those factors definitely showed up in the data, what stood out to me more was how often management came up in employee experiences.

One review in particular stuck with me. A former employee described how managers frequently came and went because they were overwhelmed and not properly trained. Over time, this made leadership feel unreliable, and ultimately influenced their decision to leave.

That insight changed how I started looking at the rest of the data.

Even when reviews didn’t explicitly say the same thing, there were consistent patterns:
- mentions of poor management  
- feeling unsupported  
- difficulty navigating processes  
- lack of a reliable support system  

It made me realize that what might initially look like a workload problem could actually be a support and leadership problem underneath.

I also started thinking more about how pressure flows through the system. If management is overwhelmed or underprepared, that pressure doesn’t disappear — it shifts downward to associates. That added layer of strain, on top of already demanding work, likely contributes to burnout and attrition.
