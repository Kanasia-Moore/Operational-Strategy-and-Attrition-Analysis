## Project 03 Reflection

This phase of the project was one of the most challenging, but also one of the most rewarding.

A large part of the work involved refining how I cleaned and structured text data before analysis. It made me realize how important preparation is when working with unstructured data. If the data isn’t properly cleaned or organized, it becomes much harder for tools like TextBlob to accurately interpret sentiment.

One of the biggest takeaways for me was the importance of research and context. Platforms like YouTube and Glassdoor provide valuable insights, but the way that data is structured directly impacts how well those insights translate during analysis.

I also saw how technical work connects back to the bigger picture. Even after applying sentiment scoring and visualizing the data, the same themes continued to show up — burnout, workload pressure, and concerns around management and support.

This reinforced the idea that tools can help quantify patterns, but understanding the context behind the data is just as important.

Overall, this phase pushed me to become more comfortable working with messy data and thinking more critically about how to prepare it for analysis.
