## Project 01 Reflection

This week gave me a much clearer understanding of how operational workflows actually function in a fulfillment environment.

What stood out to me the most is how interconnected everything is. While the entire process relies heavily on frontline execution, frontline roles also depend just as much on leadership and support structures to function effectively. This creates a cyclical system where performance is not just driven by individual tasks, but by how well each layer of the operation supports the others.

Working through the workforce structure and role mapping also showed me the importance of understanding each role in context — not just what tasks are performed, but how those tasks connect across the system. Mapping out roles, tools, and workflow relationships opened up more ways to identify where operations can break down.

One area I want to explore further is the role of leadership within these systems. My initial briefing note focused more on improving conditions for frontline workers, but I’m starting to see that leadership structure, communication, and support may play an equally important role in both performance and attrition.

This is something I want to build on in the next phase of the project.
