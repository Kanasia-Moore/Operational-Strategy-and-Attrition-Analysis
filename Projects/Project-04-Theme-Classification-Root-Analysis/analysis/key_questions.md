## Key Questions & Operational Thinking

---

### Question 1  
What recurring themes appeared most consistently within employee feedback?

### Thinking
After reviewing employee voice data and refining AI-assisted theme generation, several recurring themes consistently emerged:

- Benefits
- Burnout
- Infrastructure
- Management

While some themes reflected positive experiences, others highlighted operational pressure, physical strain, and lack of support within the workplace.

Using AI-assisted analysis helped surface these themes more efficiently, but refining them required operational context and analytical questioning to ensure the categories accurately reflected employee experiences.

### Insight
Recurring themes across reviews reflected both workplace strengths and operational pain points.

### Operational Implication
Theme classification can help identify which operational areas contribute most heavily to employee dissatisfaction and attrition.

---

### Question 2  
What did sentiment analysis reveal about the identified themes?

### Thinking
After categorizing employee reviews into themes and visualizing sentiment patterns, several differences became clear.

Benefits showed the strongest positive sentiment overall, suggesting that employees generally viewed compensation and benefits favorably.

However, themes related to burnout, infrastructure, and management showed more balanced or negative sentiment distributions.

Management-related feedback frequently referenced:
- poor communication
- lack of support
- inconsistent leadership
- unreliable guidance

Burnout and infrastructure themes often referenced:
- unrealistic pace expectations
- physical strain
- repetitive work
- insufficient recovery time

### Insight
Positive compensation alone does not appear to offset operational strain or dissatisfaction tied to support and workplace structure.

### Operational Implication
Improving retention may require organizations to focus not only on incentives and compensation, but also on workload sustainability, leadership support, and employee wellbeing.

---

### Question 3  
How did root cause analysis change the direction of the project?

### Thinking
Using root cause analysis shifted the focus from identifying symptoms to understanding how operational pressures connect throughout the system.

The 5 Why's analysis and root cause tree revealed a recurring pattern:

Operational productivity demands  
→ management strain  
→ reduced employee support  
→ burnout and disengagement  
→ attrition

This suggested that many employee concerns were not isolated incidents, but downstream effects of broader operational design decisions.

### Insight
Attrition appears to function as a system-level outcome rather than a standalone workforce issue.

### Operational Implication
Long-term operational improvement may require balancing productivity goals with workforce sustainability and support structures.

---

### Question 4  
What role does management appear to play in employee experience?

### Thinking
Management-related concerns appeared repeatedly throughout employee feedback and became more noticeable after reviews were grouped into themes.

Employees often described:
- feeling unsupported
- inconsistent communication
- difficulty navigating issues
- lack of reliable leadership

At the same time, analysis suggested that managers themselves may also be operating under significant pressure due to productivity expectations, workload demands, and limited preparation.

### Insight
Management challenges may stem not only from individual leadership issues, but from pressure embedded within the operational system itself.

### Operational Implication
Supporting managers more effectively may improve both operational performance and employee experience at the associate level.

---

### Closing Thought
This phase reinforced that operational issues are rarely isolated.

Burnout, management instability, workload pressure, and attrition appear deeply connected through the broader operational structure and support systems surrounding employees.

Understanding those relationships became a major turning point in the direction of this project.
