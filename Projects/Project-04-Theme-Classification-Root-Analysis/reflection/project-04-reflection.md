## Project 04 Reflection

This phase of the project significantly strengthened my root cause analysis skills.

At first, I approached root cause analysis as if I needed to come up with questions almost out of thin air. But as I worked through the employee voice data, I realized the answers to many of the initial questions were already present within the data itself. Once I started treating each finding as a lead into the next question, it became much easier to build meaningful follow-up questions and trace issues back to deeper operational causes.

That shift completely changed how I approached analysis.

Another major takeaway from this project was the importance of cleaning and organizing voice data before analysis. While this may sound like a small technical step, I realized it directly impacts how accurately themes, sentiment, and operational patterns are interpreted. If the data is poorly structured, the analysis becomes much less reliable.

This phase also changed how I think about AI within analytical work. I originally viewed AI more as a support tool, but I was surprised by how useful it was in helping surface themes and patterns from large amounts of employee feedback.

One of the most interesting parts of the project was comparing my own 5 Why's analysis to the AI-generated version. While many of the conclusions were similar, one framing stood out to me the most:

> “The system was optimized for output — not people.”

That became the biggest turning point for me throughout this project.

It connected many of the recurring themes together:
- burnout
- management strain
- operational pressure
- lack of support
- attrition

Rather than viewing these as isolated problems, I started seeing them as downstream effects of a broader operational structure focused heavily on productivity and efficiency.
