## Project 05: Voice Data Segmentation & Priority Identification

### Overview
This phase of the project focuses on segmenting employee voice data to better understand which operational concerns appear most urgent and actionable within the broader attrition analysis.

Building on previous theme classification and root cause analysis work, this stage shifts toward prioritization by organizing employee feedback into clearer segments and identifying which issues may have the greatest impact on employee experience and retention.

### Objective
To segment employee voice data, identify high-priority operational concerns, and determine which themes may require the most immediate attention based on frequency, sentiment, and operational impact.

### What Was Done
- Segmented employee voice data into operational concern groups
- Compared recurring themes across employee feedback segments
- Identified patterns tied to burnout, management support, operational strain, and workplace infrastructure
- Used Python to organize and analyze segmented voice data
- Evaluated which operational concerns appeared most severe or consistently referenced
- Created a friction map and supporting visuals to highlight operational pain points and workflow breakdowns
- Began prioritizing issues based on operational impact and employee experience trends

### Tools Used
- Python
- Pandas
- TextBlob
- AI-assisted analysis
- Data visualization techniques

### Key Questions Explored
- Which operational concerns appear most frequently across employee feedback?
- Which concerns show the strongest negative sentiment?
- What issues appear most connected to burnout and attrition?
- How can segmentation help prioritize operational improvements?

### Key Insights
Segmenting employee feedback made it easier to distinguish between recurring operational frustrations and identify which issues appeared most consistently connected to employee dissatisfaction.

While concerns related to workload and burnout remained highly visible, management support and operational pressure continued to emerge as major contributing factors across multiple segments.

Creating the friction map also helped visualize how operational breakdowns and pressure points interact across the workflow, making it easier to identify where strain accumulates within the system.

This phase reinforced the idea that prioritization is important within operational analysis, as not all issues carry the same level of impact on employee experience or retention.
