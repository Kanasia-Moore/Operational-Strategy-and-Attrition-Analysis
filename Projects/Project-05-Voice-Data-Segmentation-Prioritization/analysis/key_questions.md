## Key Questions & Operational Thinking

---

### Question 1  
How can segmentation and friction mapping help identify priority areas?

### Thinking
Employee voice data often contains overlapping concerns, which can make it difficult to determine which issues should be addressed first. By segmenting the data into clearer operational concern areas, it becomes easier to compare patterns across feedback and identify which issues appear most frequent, severe, or closely connected to attrition.

Creating a friction map added another layer to this process by helping visualize where operational pressure points appear within the employee experience. Instead of only seeing feedback as separate comments, the friction map helped show how concerns may connect across workflow, management support, burnout, and operational strain.

Segmentation helped organize the feedback, while the friction map helped translate those segments into visible priority areas.

### Insight
Segmentation and friction mapping create a clearer path from employee feedback to operational prioritization.

### Operational Implication
When feedback is organized and visualized, organizations can better distinguish between general frustrations and high-impact issues that may require more immediate attention.

---

### Question 2  
Which operational concerns appear most connected to burnout and attrition?

### Thinking
Across the segmented voice data, concerns related to workload, management support, operational strain, and workplace infrastructure remained closely connected to burnout and attrition.

Rather than appearing as separate issues, these concerns often overlapped. For example, workload pressure could become more difficult to manage when employees also felt unsupported by leadership or lacked clear systems for addressing concerns.

### Insight
Burnout and attrition appear to be influenced by multiple connected pressure points rather than one isolated issue.

### Operational Implication
Reducing attrition may require addressing both task-level strain and the support systems surrounding employees, especially management communication, recovery time, and workflow pressure.

---

### Question 3  
How did the friction map help visualize operational pain points?

### Thinking
Creating a friction map helped translate segmented feedback into a visual representation of where pressure appears to build within the operation.

Instead of only listing concerns, the friction map made it easier to see how different pain points may interact across the workflow and employee experience.

This helped connect voice data back to operational systems by showing where breakdowns, support gaps, or recurring frustrations may create strain.

### Insight
The friction map helped make invisible pressure points more visible and easier to interpret.

### Operational Implication
Visualizing friction can support prioritization by helping identify where intervention may have the greatest impact on reducing strain and improving workflow sustainability.

---

### Question 4  
How can prioritization support operational improvement?

### Thinking
Not every issue carries the same level of urgency or impact. Some concerns may appear frequently but have a smaller operational effect, while others may appear less often but strongly influence burnout, trust, or retention.

Prioritization helps separate what is noticeable from what is most important.

This phase helped shift the analysis toward understanding which issues should be addressed first based on their relationship to employee experience and attrition.

### Insight
Prioritization is necessary for turning analysis into action.

### Operational Implication
A strong operational strategy should focus on high-impact issues first, especially those connected to burnout, management support, and workforce sustainability.

---

### Closing Thought
This phase reinforced that employee feedback becomes more actionable when it is segmented, visualized, and prioritized.

By creating a friction map and identifying priority areas, this project moved closer to translating employee voice data into operational recommendations.
