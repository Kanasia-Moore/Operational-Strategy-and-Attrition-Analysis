## Project 05 Reflection

This phase helped me understand how segmentation can make employee voice data more actionable.

By segmenting the data, I was able to see which issues appeared more frequently across different groups and better understand where support may be needed most. Instead of looking at employee feedback as one large set of comments, segmentation helped me separate concerns into clearer categories and compare their impact.

One of the most valuable parts of this project was using impact, severity, and size to help prioritize the groups. This gave me a more structured way to determine which group should be considered high priority, rather than relying only on general observations.

Creating the friction maps also helped me think more critically about where pressure builds within each segment. It pushed me to consider not only what the friction points were, but also how those issues could affect the business overall if left untreated.

One limitation I noticed was that the data did not include much variety in job titles. The job title column mainly included associates, with little to no detail about specific roles. Having more role-specific information would have made it easier to compare pain points across different positions and create more meaningful segments.

Even with that limitation, this project helped me move closer to turning analysis into strategy. It showed me that identifying problems is only one part of the work. Prioritizing which problems to address first is just as important.

---

### Strengths
- Strengthened my ability to segment employee voice data into clearer operational groups
- Used impact, severity, and size to support priority scoring
- Created friction maps to visualize pressure points within each segment
- Connected employee feedback to potential business impact

---

### Areas for Improvement
- Work with more detailed role or job-title data when available
- Continue refining how priority scores are calculated and explained
- Strengthen the connection between segmented findings and final recommendations

---

### Looking Ahead
Moving forward, I want to use these priority findings to support stronger recommendations in the final case study.

This phase helped me see that effective operational strategy is not only about identifying where problems exist, but deciding which issues should be addressed first based on impact, urgency, and long-term business risk.
