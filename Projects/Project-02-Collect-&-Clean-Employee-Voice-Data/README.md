## Project 02: Collect & Clean Employee Voice Data

### Overview
This project introduces the human side of operations by analyzing employee voice data and uncovering patterns that may influence attrition.

Using publicly available sources such as YouTube and Glassdoor, I collected and worked with unstructured employee feedback to identify recurring themes related to workplace experience, operational challenges, workload, burnout, and support systems.

This phase builds on Project 01 by moving from operational structure to employee experience — connecting how work is designed to how employees describe the reality of working within that system.

---

### Objective
To explore how employee voice data reflects operational conditions and identify recurring themes that may contribute to burnout, dissatisfaction, and attrition risk.

---

### What Was Done
- Collected employee voice data from YouTube and Glassdoor  
- Organized raw, unstructured employee feedback for analysis  
- Cleaned and structured text data using Python  
- Conducted exploratory analysis to identify recurring themes  
- Reviewed sentiment patterns across employee experiences  
- Compared how different platforms capture employee concerns  

---

### Key Questions Explored
- What themes consistently appear in employee feedback?  
- How does employee feedback reflect day-to-day operational conditions?  
- How does sentiment reflect employee experience within operations?  
- What role does management play in employee satisfaction and retention?  
- How do employee concerns connect back to the workforce structure mapped in Project 01?  

---

### Key Insights
Recurring themes such as burnout, physical strain, workload intensity, and work-life balance challenges were consistently present across employee feedback.

However, one of the most notable patterns was the role of management. Many employees referenced poor communication, lack of support, and inconsistency in leadership. In some cases, frequent manager turnover — often due to overwhelm or lack of training — created an environment where employees felt they could not rely on leadership for guidance.

These findings suggest that attrition may not be driven solely by the nature of the work, but also by how effectively employees are supported within the operational structure.

This project also highlighted the value of employee voice data as an early signal. While public feedback can be messy and unstructured, it can still reveal patterns that help explain where operational systems may be breaking down.
