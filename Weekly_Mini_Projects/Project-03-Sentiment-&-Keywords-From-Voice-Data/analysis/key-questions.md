## Key Questions & Operational Thinking

---

### Question 1  
How does text cleaning impact the accuracy of sentiment analysis?

### Thinking  
Working with unstructured employee voice data made it clear that raw text is often inconsistent, noisy, and difficult for models to interpret.

Without proper cleaning — such as removing stopwords, standardizing text, and filtering irrelevant content — sentiment analysis tools may misinterpret the meaning of the data or produce unreliable scores.

During this process, I noticed that even small inconsistencies in formatting or wording could affect how sentiment was classified.

### Insight  
The accuracy of sentiment analysis is directly tied to how well the data is prepared.

### Operational Implication  
Investing time in proper data cleaning and preprocessing is essential for producing reliable insights. Poor data preparation can lead to misleading conclusions, even when using established tools.

---

### Question 2  
What limitations exist when using tools like TextBlob for sentiment analysis?

### Thinking  
While TextBlob provides a simple way to assign sentiment scores, it does not fully capture context, tone, or nuance in employee feedback.

For example:
- Sarcasm or frustration may not be accurately detected  
- Neutral or mixed statements may be oversimplified  
- Complex experiences may be reduced to a single score  

This became more noticeable when comparing sentiment scores to the actual content of reviews.

### Insight  
Sentiment scores provide a useful signal, but they do not replace contextual understanding.

### Operational Implication  
Sentiment analysis should be used as a supporting tool rather than a standalone measure. Combining quantitative scores with qualitative review analysis leads to more accurate interpretations.

---

### Question 3  
What patterns emerge when sentiment data is visualized?

### Thinking  
By converting sentiment scores into charts, it became easier to identify trends and distributions across employee feedback.

Visualization helped highlight:
- overall sentiment distribution  
- frequency of negative vs positive experiences  
- recurring patterns tied to previously identified themes  

However, while charts made patterns more visible, they did not explain the underlying causes on their own.

### Insight  
Visualization enhances pattern recognition, but interpretation is still required to understand the “why” behind the data.

### Operational Implication  
Data visualization is most effective when paired with contextual analysis, ensuring that insights are not just observed but properly understood.

---

### Question 4  
How do quantified sentiment results connect to previously identified themes?

### Thinking  
After applying sentiment analysis, the same themes identified in earlier stages continued to appear — particularly burnout, workload pressure, and concerns related to management and support.

Even when quantified, these patterns remained consistent, reinforcing earlier qualitative findings.

### Insight  
Quantitative analysis validated the qualitative patterns identified in earlier stages of the project.

### Operational Implication  
Combining qualitative and quantitative approaches strengthens confidence in findings and provides a more complete understanding of operational issues.

---

### Closing Thought  
This phase reinforced that technical tools are only as effective as the data and context behind them.

Accurate insights require a balance between:
- clean and well-structured data  
- reliable analytical tools  
- and a clear understanding of the real-world context the data represents
